About Me

Biography

I am a front‑end developer and researcher committed to creating inclusive digital experiences. My academic training in computer science has given me a strong foundation in software engineering and user‑centred design. Over the years I have taught web development courses, worked on collaborative research projects, and consulted for organisations seeking to improve their digital accessibility. When I design and build websites, I strive to use semantic HTML and progressive enhancement so that everyone can access the content, regardless of their device or assistive technology.

Goals in this Programme

My primary objective in participating in this program is to refine my proficiency with modern front‑end frameworks while preserving a focus on accessible and maintainable code. Specifically, I aim to

  • deeply understand responsive design patterns and how they can be applied without relying on heavy frameworks,
  • develop robust client‑side validation techniques that complement server‑side checks, ensuring that forms are inclusive and user friendly,
  • explore emerging CSS features such as container queries to make layouts more adaptable, and
  • contribute to the broader conversation on digital accessibility through research and teaching.

Areas of Low Confidence

Despite my experience, there remain areas where I seek to grow. I occasionally struggle with intricate animations and transitions, particularly when balancing performance with visual richness. In addition, while I understand the fundamentals of progressive web apps (PWAs), I am less confident in implementing service workers and offline caching strategies. Part of my professional development plan involves dedicating time to master these concepts so that I can build applications that are both engaging and resilient. Recognising gaps in one’s knowledge is the first step toward addressing them.

Note to Future Self

As you continue your journey, remember that technology evolves rapidly but the fundamentals remain constant. Prioritise clear semantics and accessibility from the outset of every project. Do not let the allure of new libraries distract you from the core principles of web development: clean markup, readable stylesheets, and unobtrusive scripts. Keep a research journal to document challenges and breakthroughs; future you will appreciate the record of growth.

Extra Thoughts

Participating in this stage of the program has reinforced my belief that accessible design is not an afterthought but a core requirement of professional web development. Building a contact form that validates input while communicating errors through ARIA attributes illustrates how thoughtful engineering can make a profound difference to users. The process also served as a reminder that responsive design is achieved through careful consideration of layout, typography, and spacing rather than a collection of device‑specific breakpoints.
